Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843)

GN-WAAS is registered with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana and processes personal data in full compliance with Act 843. This page explains how the eight data protection principles are implemented within the GN-WAAS platform.

1. The Eight Data Protection Principles

GN-WAAS implements all eight principles of Act 843 as follows:

Principle 1

Accountability

The Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation is the registered data controller. A designated Data Protection Officer oversees compliance.

Principle 2

Lawfulness of Processing

All processing is authorised by statutory mandate (Act 1151, PURC regulations) or explicit user consent at account creation.

Principle 3

Specification of Purpose

Data is collected exclusively for water audit, NRW analysis, and GRA VAT compliance. No secondary commercial use.

Principle 4

Compatibility of Purpose

Data is never used for purposes incompatible with the original audit mandate without fresh consent or legal authority.

Principle 5

Quality of Information

Billing data is sourced from GWL's authoritative read-only replica. Field data is GPS-locked and OCR-verified for accuracy.

Principle 6

Openness

This privacy notice and data protection statement are publicly available. Data subjects are informed at point of collection.

Principle 7

Data Security Safeguards

AES-256 encryption at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit, RBAC with Row-Level Security, immutable audit trail, NITA-certified hosting.

Principle 8

Data Subject Participation

Data subjects may request access, correction, or deletion of their personal data via the Data Protection Officer.

2. Registration & Accountability

GN-WAAS is registered with the Data Protection Commission of Ghana as a data controller under Act 843. The Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation bears ultimate accountability for all personal data processed through the system.

A Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA) was conducted prior to system launch, identifying and mitigating risks associated with processing water account holder data and field officer location data.

3. Data Categories & Sensitivity

Standard Personal Data

  • System user names, email addresses, employee IDs, phone numbers
  • Water account holder names and billing addresses
  • Consumption records and billing history

Sensitive / Special Category Data

  • Location data: GPS coordinates of field officers during active jobs (processed under statutory mandate)
  • Biometric-adjacent: Device ID binding for field officers (not biometric data per se, but treated with equivalent care)

GN-WAAS does not process health data, racial or ethnic origin data, political opinions, religious beliefs, or criminal record data.

4. Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest; TLS 1.3 in transit for all API communications
  • Access Control: Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) with PostgreSQL Row-Level Security (RLS) — users can only access data within their assigned district and role scope
  • Audit Trail: All data access and modifications are logged in an immutable audit table. Logs cannot be altered or deleted by any user, including administrators
  • Authentication: bcrypt password hashing (cost factor 12); multi-factor authentication for administrators
  • Infrastructure: Hosted on NITA-certified data centres within Ghana; no data leaves Ghanaian jurisdiction
  • Penetration Testing: Annual third-party security assessments

5. Data Subject Rights

Under Act 843, data subjects have the right to:

  • Access: Obtain confirmation of whether data is held and receive a copy — respond within 30 days
  • Rectification: Correct inaccurate personal data — respond within 21 days
  • Erasure: Request deletion where data is no longer necessary (subject to 7-year statutory audit retention)
  • Objection: Object to processing causing unwarranted substantial damage or distress
  • Restriction: Request restriction of processing pending resolution of a dispute

Submit requests to: dataprotection@mwhwr.gov.gh

6. Cross-Border Transfer Restrictions

In compliance with Section 43 of Act 843, GN-WAAS does not transfer personal data to any country outside Ghana unless:

  • The receiving country provides an adequate level of data protection as determined by the Data Protection Commission; or
  • The transfer is necessary for the performance of a contract with the data subject; or
  • The data subject has given explicit consent

Current status: All GN-WAAS data is stored and processed exclusively within Ghana on NITA-certified infrastructure. No international transfers are made.

7. Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach, GN-WAAS will:

  • Notify the Data Protection Commission within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach
  • Notify affected data subjects without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms
  • Document all breaches in the internal breach register, including remediation actions taken

To report a suspected data breach: security@mwhwr.gov.gh
