1. Who We Are

The Ghana National Water Audit & Assurance System (GN-WAAS) is a sovereign government platform operated by the Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation of the Republic of Ghana, in coordination with the Public Utilities Regulatory Commission (PURC) and the Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA).

GN-WAAS is the data controller for all personal data processed through this system. Our registered address is:

Data Controller

Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation

P.O. Box M212, Accra, Ghana

Website: mwhwr.gov.gh

Email: dataprotection@mwhwr.gov.gh

2. Data We Collect

2.1 System Users (Government & Utility Personnel)

  • Full name, email address, phone number, employee ID
  • Organisation and role (e.g., GRA Auditor, Field Officer)
  • Device ID (for field officers — anti-sharing enforcement)
  • GPS location data during active field jobs
  • Login timestamps and session activity logs
  • Audit actions performed within the system

2.2 Water Account Holders (Billing Data)

  • GWL account number and billing address
  • Consumption records and billing history (read-only mirror from GWL)
  • Meter GPS coordinates and service category
  • Anomaly flags and audit case outcomes

2.3 Field Evidence

  • Meter reading photographs (OCR-processed)
  • GPS-locked field visit records
  • Digital signatures on audit submissions

Note: GN-WAAS does not collect payment card details, national ID numbers, or biometric data. Billing data is sourced exclusively from GWL's read-only replica database.

3. How We Use Your Data

We process personal data solely for the following statutory purposes:

  • Audit & Compliance: Detecting non-revenue water losses, billing fraud, and VAT evasion under Act 1151
  • Field Operations: Dispatching and tracking field officers for meter verification
  • Regulatory Reporting: Generating GRA-signed compliance reports for PURC and the Ministry
  • System Security: Preventing unauthorised access and detecting anomalous system behaviour
  • Audit Trail: Maintaining an immutable record of all audit actions for legal accountability

We do not use personal data for commercial marketing, profiling for non-audit purposes, or sale to third parties.

4. Legal Basis for Processing

GN-WAAS processes personal data under the following legal bases as defined in the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843):

  • Public Task / Legal Obligation: Processing is necessary for the performance of a task carried out in the public interest and in the exercise of official authority vested in the Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation
  • Statutory Mandate: Act 1151 (Electronic VAT) requires audit and verification of water utility billing records
  • Consent: System users provide explicit consent upon account creation for processing of their employment and activity data

5. Data Sharing & Disclosure

Personal data may be shared with the following authorised parties only:

  • Ghana Revenue Authority (GRA): Audit reports and VAT compliance data as required by Act 1151
  • Public Utilities Regulatory Commission (PURC): NRW analysis and district water balance reports
  • Ghana Water Limited (GWL): Audit case outcomes and billing reclassification decisions
  • Ministry of Finance: Aggregated revenue recovery statistics (no individual account data)
  • Law Enforcement: Where required by court order or statutory obligation

No international transfers: All GN-WAAS data is stored on NITA-certified data centres within the Republic of Ghana. No personal data is transferred outside Ghana.

6. Data Retention

  • Audit records & evidence: 7 years (statutory requirement for financial audit records)
  • Field officer GPS logs: 2 years
  • System access logs: 3 years
  • User account data: Duration of employment + 2 years
  • Billing mirror data: 5 years from billing date

After the retention period, data is securely deleted or anonymised in accordance with NITA data disposal guidelines.

7. Your Rights

Under the Data Protection Act, 2012 (Act 843), you have the following rights:

  • Right of Access: Request a copy of personal data held about you
  • Right to Rectification: Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of data where no longer necessary (subject to statutory retention requirements)
  • Right to Object: Object to processing where it causes unwarranted harm
  • Right to Restriction: Request restriction of processing pending a dispute

To exercise any of these rights, contact our Data Protection Officer at dataprotection@mwhwr.gov.gh. We will respond within 30 days.

8. Security Measures

GN-WAAS implements the following technical and organisational security measures:

  • All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
  • Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) with Row-Level Security (RLS) enforced at the database layer
  • Multi-factor authentication for all system administrators
  • Immutable audit trail — all data modifications are logged and cannot be altered
  • NITA-certified data centre with ISO 27001 security framework
  • Regular penetration testing and security audits
  • Device authentication for field officers (device ID binding)

9. Cookies & Tracking

GN-WAAS uses only essential session cookies required for system authentication. We do not use:

  • Third-party advertising or tracking cookies
  • Analytics platforms that share data with third parties
  • Social media tracking pixels

Session cookies expire when you close your browser or after 24 hours of inactivity.
