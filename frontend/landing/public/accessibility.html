Accessibility Statement

GN-WAAS is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all users, including persons with disabilities, in accordance with Ghana's digital inclusion standards.

Standard: WCAG 2.1 Level AA Last Reviewed: 10 March 2026 Next Review: 10 September 2026

GN-WAAS aims to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 at Level AA. This statement was prepared on 10 March 2026 and reflects the current accessibility status of the platform.

1. Our Commitment

The Ministry of Water Resources & Sanitation is committed to making GN-WAAS accessible to all authorised users, including those with visual, auditory, motor, or cognitive disabilities. We believe that government digital services must be usable by everyone.

This commitment extends to all GN-WAAS portals:

  • Landing Page (gnwaas-landing.vercel.app)
  • Operations & Audit Portal (gnwaas-admin.vercel.app)
  • GWL Case Management Portal (gnwaas-gwl.vercel.app)
  • Regulatory Oversight Portal (gnwaas-authority.vercel.app)
  • Field Officer Mobile App (gnwaas-mobile.vercel.app)

2. Conformance Status

GN-WAAS is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 Level AA. Partial conformance means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard. We are actively working to achieve full conformance.

✅ Conformant

Colour Contrast

All text meets minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio against backgrounds.

✅ Conformant

Keyboard Navigation

All interactive elements are accessible via keyboard (Tab, Enter, Space, Arrow keys).

✅ Conformant

Text Alternatives

All non-decorative images have descriptive alt text. Icons include aria-labels.

✅ Conformant

Form Labels

All form inputs have associated labels. Error messages are descriptive and programmatically associated.

⚠️ Partial

Data Tables

Most data tables have headers. Complex audit tables are being updated with full ARIA table roles.

⚠️ Partial

Map Accessibility

The DMA heatmap provides a text alternative summary. Full screen-reader map navigation is planned.

🔵 Planned

PDF Reports

GRA-signed PDF reports will be updated to include tagged PDF structure for screen readers by Q3 2026.

🔵 Planned

Mobile App (Flutter)

Full Flutter accessibility semantics (Semantics widgets) are planned for the field officer app in v2.0.

3. Known Accessibility Issues

The following known issues are being actively addressed:

  • DMA Heatmap (Admin Portal): The interactive map does not fully support screen reader navigation. A text-based district summary table is provided as an alternative.
  • PDF Reports: Downloaded PDF reports are not yet tagged for screen reader compatibility. We aim to resolve this by Q3 2026.
  • Complex Data Tables: Some audit event tables with merged cells may not be fully navigable by screen readers. We are refactoring these tables.
  • Mobile App: The Flutter field officer app has limited accessibility semantics in the current version. This is a known limitation being addressed in v2.0.

If you encounter an accessibility barrier not listed here, please report it using the contact details in Section 6. We aim to respond within 5 working days.

4. Technical Specifications

GN-WAAS relies on the following technologies for accessibility:

  • HTML5 — semantic markup with appropriate landmark roles
  • WAI-ARIA — ARIA roles, states, and properties for dynamic content
  • CSS3 — responsive design supporting 200% text zoom without horizontal scrolling
  • React 18 — component-based UI with focus management for modal dialogs
  • Flutter 3.19 — mobile app with Semantics widget support (partial)

The portals have been tested with the following browsers and assistive technologies:

  • Chrome 120+ with NVDA 2024.1 (Windows)
  • Safari 17+ with VoiceOver (macOS)
  • Firefox 121+ with JAWS 2024 (Windows)
  • Chrome for Android with TalkBack

5. Assistive Technology Support

GN-WAAS is designed to work with the following assistive technologies:

  • Screen readers: NVDA, JAWS, VoiceOver, TalkBack
  • Magnification software: ZoomText, Windows Magnifier, macOS Zoom
  • Voice control: Dragon NaturallySpeaking, Windows Speech Recognition
  • Switch access: Keyboard-only navigation is fully supported across all portals
  • High contrast mode: All portals support Windows High Contrast mode

6. Feedback & Contact

We welcome feedback on the accessibility of GN-WAAS. If you experience any accessibility barriers or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us:

GN-WAAS Accessibility Team

Email: accessibility@mwhwr.gov.gh

Phone: +233 (0) 302 664 697

Response time: Within 5 working days

When contacting us, please include:

  • The URL of the page where you encountered the issue
  • A description of the accessibility barrier
  • The assistive technology you are using (if applicable)
  • Your preferred contact method for our response

7. Enforcement Procedure

If you are not satisfied with our response to your accessibility complaint, you may escalate to:

  • National Information Technology Agency (NITA) — responsible for digital accessibility standards in Ghana government systems: nita.gov.gh
  • Commission on Human Rights and Administrative Justice (CHRAJ) — for complaints about discrimination in access to public services: chraj.gov.gh
